# PROVING GROUND | The Remodel
## Copy Traceback + Strategic Context

This document does two things:
1. Establishes the strategic context the page was built for (awareness, attention, platform, action)
2. Traces every key line of copy back to its specific source in the customer research

The page is not a generic landing page. It's engineered for a specific buyer, in a specific psychological state, in a specific attention mode, on a specific platform. Without that frame, the copy choices look arbitrary. With it, every choice is justified.

## STRATEGIC CONTEXT

### Target Buyer (one-line summary)

Married woman aged 38 to 58 in an affluent Ridgeview household, juggling work, kids, and the home, who has been dreaming about her renovation on Pinterest for months but is afraid of getting burned by the wrong contractor.

### Awareness Level: **Solution Aware**

She knows she needs a remodel. She knows contractors exist. She has been mentally designing this kitchen for months or years. She is not asking "what is a kitchen remodel" — she is asking "who do I trust with mine."

**Evidence:** Reviews mention going through 2 to 3 contractors before finding Dave. The Pinterest board exists. The budget is mentally allocated. She is past the problem stage and deep into provider evaluation.

### Attention Mode: **Mostly Passive (with a small Active tail)**

90% of her buying journey is passive. She is scrolling Instagram at night, saving Pinterest pins, watching HGTV, asking friends. She is not Googling.

The active search ("kitchen remodel ridgeview") is the last 10% — she Googles a name she already heard from a friend or saw on a yard sign, just to verify the business exists before she calls.

**This is why Dave's previous Google ad failed.** He paid to capture the last 10% with no presence in the first 90%. By the time she Googles, she has already chosen who she is calling. The Google ad never had a chance to build the relationship.

### Platform Strategy

| Platform | Role | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Instagram (paid + organic)** | PRIMARY traffic source | This is where she lives at night. Local design accounts, before/after reels, contractor pages tagged by clients. Solution-aware + passive = social discovery. The landing page is built to convert traffic from this channel. |
| **Facebook (paid)** | Secondary traffic source | Local Ridgeview mom groups and neighborhood groups where renovation recommendations get crowdsourced. Word-of-mouth amplifier. |
| **Google Search (organic + branded)** | Capture only, not generate | She'll Google after seeing a friend's post or a yard sign. The page needs to close the deal, not generate the lead. |
| **Yard signs + truck wraps** | Underrated real-world driver | "Drove past a job site" closes deals. Real-world Ridgeview presence is essentially free advertising to the exact neighborhood she lives in. Drives branded Google traffic that lands here. |

### Funnel Type Selected: **Differentiation Funnel**

From the Awareness x Attention matrix: **Solution Aware + Passive = Differentiation Funnel.**

The structure: Social ad → "Why I'm different" landing page → Consultation booking.

The page does not need to teach her what a kitchen remodel is (she already knows). The page does not need to convince her she wants one (she's been wanting it for two years). The page needs to convince her **Dave is the right version of the solution she already wants.**

That is why the page leads with differentiation ("Most contractors design your kitchen in their head before you finish your sentence. I don't.") rather than education or desire-building.

### Desired Action

**Book a free 60-minute "Kitchen Table Consult" with Dave at her home.**

Not "request a quote." Not "schedule an estimate." The framing matters: he sits at her kitchen table for an hour, listens, asks about her vision, walks the space, and tells her honestly what's possible. No quote pressure. No pitch.

**Why this action:** Dave already wins 70% of the time once a prospect reaches the consult. The job of the marketing is not to convince her to hire him — it is to get her to that table.

### Threshold Gap Analysis

| Threshold | Starts at | Needs to reach | Page strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Desire** | 8/10 | 8/10 (already high) | Anchor and amplify, don't sell |
| **Certainty** | 3/10 | 7/10 | Mechanism reframe, real proof, specific commitments |
| **Trust** | 2/10 | 8/10 | Face on the brand, daily-update promise, real client name + photo, zero-commitment offer |

**Biggest gap: TRUST.** The page is engineered primarily to lower fear, not to build excitement. Every section is calibrated against this priority.
